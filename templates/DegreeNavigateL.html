2. Dirichlet L-functions associated to a primitive {{ KNOWL('character.dirichlet', title='Dirichlet character') }}

{{ KNOWL('lfunction.dirichlet', title='Dirichlet L-functions') }} were introduced by {{ KNOWL('bio.dirichlet', title='Dirichlet') }} in the mid-1800s as a tool to study prime numbers in arithmetic progressions.
These L-functions have a functional equation of the form \[ \Lambda(s,\chi) = q^{s/2} \Gamma_{\mathbb R} (s+a) L(s,\chi) = \varepsilon_\chi \overline{\Lambda}(1-s), \] where $q$ is the conductor of $\chi$.

Automorphic Objects

There are two known types of primitive degree 2 L-functions that arise from automorphic objects:
  1. $L(s, f)$: the L-function of a holomorphic newform in $S_k(\Gamma_0(N))$
  2. $L(s, f)$: the L-function of a Maass cusp form on $\Gamma_0(N)$

Geometric Objects

There is one known type of primitive degree 2 L-function that arises from a geometric object:
  • $L(s, E)$: the L-function of an elliptic curve $E$ over $\mathbb{Q}$.

Symmetric Square $L$-functions of Elliptic Curves over $\mathbb{Q}$

Given an $L$-function of an Elliptic Curve of conductor $N$, the $n$-th symmetric power $L$-functions is defined by the euler product \begin{equation} \prod_{p\nmid N} \prod_{j=0}^n \left(1 - \frac{\alpha_p^{j} \beta^{n-j}_p}{p^s} \right)^{-1} \times \prod_{p|N} L_p(s) \end{equation}
{{contents[1]|safe}}

In general, if an $L$-function is given by \begin{equation} L(s)= \prod_{p\not\in S} \prod_{j=1}^r \left (1- \frac{\alpha_j}{p^s}\right)^{-1} \times \prod_{p \in S} L_p(s) \end{equation} then the $n$-th symmetric power $L$-function is given by \begin{equation} L(s,\mathrm{sym}^n) = \prod_{p\not\in S}\prod_{m} \left( 1- \frac{m(\alpha_1,\dotsc,\alpha_r)}{p^s} \right)^{-1} \times \prod_{p\in S} L_p(s,\mathrm{sym}^n). \end{equation} The product is over all degree-$n$ monomials $m$ in $r$ variables.
