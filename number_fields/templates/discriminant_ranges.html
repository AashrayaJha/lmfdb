The database consists of fields from three sources:

  1. The PARI database from the Bordeaux PARI group
  2. Additional totally real fields of degrees from 6 to 10 computed by John Voight.
  3. Additional fields from John Jones-David Roberts database.

Details of the fields contained in the database

  1. PARI database: the database is complete for the discriminant ranges shown. It is possible that they are complete for larger ranges which depend on the signature, but this is not shown here.

  3. The Jones-Roberts database provides complete lists of fields satisfying a variety of conditions.

    The degree of a field is given by \(n\). {% set row_class = cycler("odd", "even") %}
    Fields unramified outside \(\{2,3\}\) with \(n\leq 7\)
    Fields ramified at only one prime \(p\) with \(p<200\) with \(n\leq 9\)
    Fields ramified at only two primes \(p\lt q \leq 5\) with \(n\leq 8\)

    The remaining cases, the bound depends on the Galois group. Galois groups are given in the form \(n\)T\(t\) where \(n\) is the degree and \(t\) it the T-number. The bound \(B\) is for the root discriminant, so a bound of \(B\) means the discriminant \(D\) satisfies \(|D|\leq B^n\).
